My Passion: Swimming

My presentation

The History of Swimming

Swimming has ancient origins dating back to Antiquity, with Egyptian paintings depicting this practice. In the 18th century, Britain witnessed the emergence of modern competitive swimming, with the introduction of rules and competitions. The 19th century saw the evolution of swimming techniques, including the crawl. In the 20th century, swimming experienced increasing internationalization and became a major sport at the Olympic Games. Today, it remains a prestigious Olympic sport and a recreational activity accessible to a wide audience, illustrating a remarkable evolution from survival skills to a global phenomenon.

My Experience in Swimming

My experience in the field of swimming began at a very young age, I started this sport at the age of 4 and continued until I was 13. This sport helped me both physically and mentally to develop. With my best friend, we competed together, and although he was better than me, he became the champion of Alsace.

Why I Stopped Swimming

I stopped swimming due to several issues, with one of the main problems being my schedule. As my timetable became increasingly hectic, the time to get home became too tight for me to attend my training sessions. However, that wasn't the only factor. A year before my decision to stop, there was a change in the coaching staff, and I didn't appreciate the new coach. Therefore, I decided to quit swimming.
